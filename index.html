making my blog image

Oct 28th, 2018

It took me 2 hours and 17 layers in paint.net to create that.

Art's tough. What led to this?

Today, I decided I would finally make a blog. But what would I write about? I started pondering various disconnected topics, from how I'd introduce myself to my current life situation. I settled on writing about how I wanted to change my life by minimizing my distractions, embracing mindfulness, and getting work done. I was in a state of flow, cherishing my newfound ability to hold a schedule and the accountability of a truly productive citizen to society. Then...

A site icon? Why, let's drop everything we planned and try to make something adorable! That's clearly the wisest use of my time!

I started with sketches.

Followed by more sketches.

And more.

In the second and third image, I was constraining the head to the size of 2 by 2 cells on the grid paper. I had my dimensions set up and I felt that with these drawings done, the final product shouldn't take too long, or require too much adjustment, right?

I first setup the paint.net document to mimic my grid paper, since I realized how much I relied on it to keep consistent proportions. This grid was made by building a 100-width square and copy pasting it until it filled the page. Once that was made, I moved to adding extra lines to help me figure out a few more details, like head shape and final image size. Armed with Wacom Bamboo I pressed forward.

I drew 4 crude passes, the first using the gridlines and each pass using the last pass as reference. The idea was that it would progressively get better, but honestly I'm not sure whether I like pass 3 or 4 looking at it now.

Making the final drawing over it all would turn out to be the most time consuming part. I separated the body into numerous layers and while I'd like to detail that process more, it's the only part of the process I didn't save as I was just so done with it and wanted to move on. This is what the image looked like in finality, though I decided to get rid of the gridlines and the Comic Sans text for the webpage as WordPress already supplied a title bar for me to put that text in.

I'm not an artist, and I think that this probably would have been produced much faster had I more skill and more acceptance over tiny flukes in detail. I spent a good 30 minutes using the paintbrush tool to carefully smoothen out the lines so that they would look like they were vectorized. In hindsight, vector would have been much faster. However, it's passable, I think! A programmer can make decent enough art if they just put in the time and effort on each and every piece. One of many major differences is that a more well-practiced artist would probably make my 2 hour work in 5 minutes, and produce something of far greater quality in 2 hours. Time is everything.

But hey, I got a topic to write about, and a lot of pictures to boot! How's that for a first blog post? Next time, I'll actually write about coding, habits, motivation, effective altruism, or one of those topics I chose to actually dedicate this blog for. I can't promise it'll have this many pictures, unless I start making mini-comics to go with my fleeting thoughts.

...you know what? That sounds like a good idea! See you later on the next post!
